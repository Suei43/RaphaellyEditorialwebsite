UPDATED - AUGUST 22, 2022

What is Leadership?

Leadership is a practical universal phenomenon. There is no society without a leader neither is there any functional group without a discernible leader. Leadership is critical to the maintenance of order, growth and attainment of purpose. The world allover is confronted with leadership crisis at the political level. Quality, selfless and disciplined leaders are in short supply in Africa and elsewhere. No country with selfless leaders should be characterized by poverty, insecurity, food supply inadequacy, high rate unemployment and rising inequality. A true leader inspires followers, and is concerned about their welfare.
Honest leaders do not exploit their followers. Honest political leaders do not subject the masses to misery and dictatorship. Every sphere of human life in society has discernible leadership. For the society at large, however, political leadership is apparent at the level of political governance. A comparative review of leadership trends on all the continents of the world will reveal that Africa is at the lowest scale of leadership weighting. Why is this so?
Leadership is diverse in nature and types, but a common element in the meaning of leadership is the prominent showing of the man or woman in the forefront of affairs. This individual tends to provide a formula to the solution of a problem. The individual appears to demonstrate courage sufficient to motivate others to pursue a cause in an attempt to change the status quo. In essence, this perceived leader tends to identify with those he/she gathers around. There must therefore be a common pursuit. On no account then should the perceived leader deviate from the main purpose or dump the followers. Any action taken by the leader outside the reason that attracted the followers in the first instance is likened to deceit.
Most political leaders in Africa, having attained their latent interest (hidden agenda), reveal their true identity, and begin to dictate situations to compound the problems under which the followers live. A leader should be totally transparent and committed to the cause of followers. This quality of transparency is lacking among African leaders. In many cases, they nurse a hidden agenda of which their followers are never aware until the agenda begins to play out. True leadership is about empathy, sympathy and sacrifice to advance society development. A record of leadership history in Africa from the time of independence of most African nations till now is replete with insincere, poor and corrupt leadership. Mention can only be made of few leaders committed to transparent ideology and selfless pursuit of liberation of the masses from squalor. It is time to begin to invest in leadership studies.
