UPDATED - AUGUST 29, 2022

Following the contributions of Great Minds

In times past, philosophers were reputed to be great minds. This is not only because they left legacies, exposed knowledge and shared wisdom, but largely, they influenced behavior in society. Their writings and thoughts opened up new areas of studies and resulted in the advancement of learning. Today, while not much attention is given to philosophers, and of course, there are many self-acclaimed and society-recognized philosophers as of old, there is enough evidence in modern libraries of other great minds.
Great minds are individuals who devote their time, attention and resources to a project, cause and study all their lives to transform human life and society for the better. History records across all fields of human endeavor individuals who are today recognized as great minds. Great minds suggest ideas, discuss and translate them into practical reality to solve society problems. In the fields of management, computer science, mathematics and leadership among others, great minds have left some legacies.
In the management of organizations for instance, what is currently known as modern management as a field of study is traced to Peter Drucker, now reputed as the father of modern management. His efforts and those of others led to the articulation of management as a professional field of study and thought-provoking academic domain. Although there could be some argument about giving credit to Peter Drucker, it is evident that he promoted the basis of what developed into management study in the United States. His revolutionary thoughts about organizations, native wisdom and practical approach to management issues in corporate organizations have extended the frontiers of management studies.
