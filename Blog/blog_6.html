A NUGGET FOR LEADERS

UPDATED - DECEMBER 28, 2022

Leaders are identified with certain features that make them stand out. When an individual assumes a leadership role in any sphere, such individual ought to be distinct in relating with others on a note of transparency. But because of surrounding followers and oppositions, leaders in every sphere of life must learn to discern, and not totally depend on what people tell them. Good leaders don't believe everything until it is verified, or they see things unfold in their presence. When they see things unfold, they are sure of having witnessed them, and can confirm the reality.
There are sycophants around most leaders. Sycophants only want to impress the leader, and are merely interested in personal gains. They feed the leader with information that will lure him/her into their camp. Every leader with a sound vision may be derailed if he/she allows sycophants to influence his/her decisions. A leader must learn to study the character and pulse of people around. Good leaders watch occurrences in the environment, make consultations with professionals in the field, and are willing to the laws of flexibility and rigidity when situations demand their necessity.
A leader need not be totally rigid or flexible. Situations arise that require shifts towards each of the laws or blending of both in application. However, whether or not a leader is successful is known by what people say of such leaders. Furthermore, what people say about a leader is informed by how much influence or otherwise the leader has made on them. Leaders can influence people's welfare, personal development, growth, vision, patriotism, motivation and national development among others. Leaders can also frustrate people's socio-economic welfare through self-centredness and unethical practices.
A good leader must interact with his/her followers or the members he/she is privileged to serve in a group or organization. Good leaders gain better advantage when they study social problems and pursue policies that address people's needs. A leader must nonetheless stick to discipline, stand for discipline and translate it to action and results. This does not imply that a bit of flexibility should not be mixed with discipline once in a while, especially where there is cause for it. Without discipline, leaders can achieve no enduring results. Discipline is the foundation for leadership results.
