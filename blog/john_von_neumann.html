One of Great Minds: John von Neumann

John von Neumann was a man whose legacy could still be seen in many fields of study. He combined two nationalities being originally known as a Hungarian, but was to be more identified as an American. He was a mathematician, physicist, computer scientist and engineer. By every academic standard, he was a genius. Modern day students of computer science will find his works invaluable. His innovative and creative skills resulted in the development of theories, machines and designs that are today credited to his name.
During his life time, he was found to be a colleague of Albert Einstein, another genius of all time. Although he was a man of many parts, John von Neumannâ€™s contributions to computer science and mathematics are profound. Most of his works find application in solving problems for the advancement of man and society. Even though he laid the foundation for the development of post-war economic theory by embarking on a paper titled: multi-sectoral growth model, his works in other fields laid the foundation for further advancement by other scholars and researchers.
John von Neumann lived from 1903 to 1957, and made a strong mark by earning two doctoral degrees, each in mathematics and chemistry respectively. This achievement is proof of his mental prowess, creativity and foresight. A genius like von Neumann had no hiding place. By Birth, he was linked to both Hungary and Israel, but his inventiveness provided a platform to switch nationality thus becoming an American citizen. President Harry S. Truman recognized his quintessential value and appointed him as one of his advisors on atomic energy.
There is always a genius in every generation. But the nurture of any emerging genius is dependent on a conducive environment which is believed to allow the genius find appropriate expression. People reputed to attract the character of a genius ought to be documented and celebrated as a source of inspiration to others in society.
